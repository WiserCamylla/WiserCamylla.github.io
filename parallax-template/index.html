What is Impressionism?

Impressionism is a style or movement in painting originating in France in the 1860s, characterized by a concern with depicting the visual impression of the moment, especially in terms of the shifting effect of light and color.

Where Can I See Impressionism?

Some of the more well-known impressionistic artists include Claude Monet, Berthe Morisot, and Edgar Degas. You can see their works in several museums, such as Le Louvre, The Met, and the Guggenheim.

brush

What Techniques are Used in Impressionism?

Artists use thick, quick brush strokes with oil or acrylic paint to capture the overall idea of an object, instead of the object's details themselves. They also would work with lighter paints, and develop contrast through those colors instead of using strong dark colors to develop contrast.

"Everyone discusses my art and pretends to understand, as if it were necessary to understand, when it is simply necessary to love." - Claude Monet

Beautiful Impressionism Paintings You Should Look At

Although there are many artists who practice Impressionism, The most well known include Edgar Dagas, Claude Monet, Camille Pissarro, and Pierre-August Renoir. If you want to observe scenery paintings, Poppies by Claude Monet and The Boulavard Montmatre at Night by Camille Pissarro are beautiful pieces. If you wanted to focus on portraits and objects, The Absinthe Drinker by Edgar Degas and Portrait of Mademoiselle Irene Cahen d'Anvers by Pierre-August Renoir are pieces that will truly inspire you.

"It is important to express oneself... provided the feelings are real and are taken from your own experience." - Berthe Morisot
I'm Trying to Start Painting Impressionistic Paintings. Where Do I Start?

Getting your own set of oil or acrylic paint is a great start. Stores such as Blick Art Materials will have everything you need and more. Making sure you have the right sort of brushes is also a plus, and will make your work more controllable and of higher quality. After that, go to a few museums! Analyze the techniques of several impressionistic artists to develop your own technique.
